⚠ Action Required
What does this mean in plain terms?
The DHI portal currently lets teachers and students use the same computers. If a teacher saves their password in the browser, a student can easily open the teacher account and change attendance records or marks — with no warning or detection. 2 out of 3 risks identified are Critical, meaning this can happen right now with no technical skill required.

R1 and R2 are in the worst possible position — high impact and high chance of happening.
What needs to be done
01
Add a second login step (MFA)
Even if a password is stolen or seen, the attacker still can't log in without a one-time code sent to the teacher's phone.
Fixes: R1, R2
02
Log every change in attendance and marks
Every edit should record who made it, when, and from which device. This makes tampering visible and traceable.
Fixes: R2
03
Only teachers can edit — students can only view
Set up role-based permissions so that students physically cannot modify any records, even if they somehow access an account.
Fixes: R3
04
Auto logout after 15 minutes of no activity
On shared lab computers, a teacher who forgets to log out leaves their account open. Auto-timeout closes it automatically.
Fixes: R1
05
Block the browser from saving passwords
The login form should be set to prevent browsers from offering to save the password. Lab computers should also be configured to block saved credentials.
Fixes: R1
06
Require strong, regularly changed passwords
Passwords should be at least 10 characters and changed every 3 months. Weak passwords are easy to guess once a pattern is spotted.
Fixes: R3
